# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

Welcome to the central documentation hub for OctoAcme's project management processes. This README serves as the entry point to all process guides, templates, and role definitions used across the organization.

## Overview

OctoAcme follows a structured, iterative project management approach that emphasizes customer value, clear ownership, and data-driven decision-making. The organization operates through a five-phase project lifecycle: Initiation, Planning, Execution, Release, and Retrospective. Each project begins with a one-pager that defines the problem statement, SMART objectives, success metrics, and key stakeholders. Projects move through formal decision gates where sponsors and product leads assess readiness before advancing to the next phase. This disciplined framework ensures alignment across cross-functional teams while maintaining flexibility for iterative delivery.

The team structure at OctoAcme centers around three core roles working in close collaboration. **Project Managers** coordinate delivery activities, manage timelines, risks, and dependencies, and maintain transparent communication with stakeholders through weekly status updates and risk registers. **Product Managers** define what gets built by prioritizing the backlog, establishing acceptance criteria, and measuring outcomes against customer and business value. **Developers** implement features while participating in design reviews, writing tests, and helping identify technical risks. This clear separation of responsibilities—with PMs focusing on "how and when," PdMs on "what and why," and developers on "how technically"—prevents overlap while encouraging collaboration through daily standups, weekly delivery syncs, and regular demos.

Communication and risk management are deeply integrated into OctoAcme's processes. The team maintains a formal Risk Register that tracks identification, impact assessment, mitigation plans, and ownership for each risk. Escalation paths are clearly defined, moving from team-level triage in standups to PM coordination with product leads, and finally to sponsor-level intervention for business-critical issues. Stakeholder communication follows a predictable cadence with weekly PM-PdM syncs, twice-weekly team standups, and monthly stakeholder updates. Every communication artifact—whether a weekly status template or incident report—follows standardized formats that highlight progress, blockers, risks, and decisions needed, ensuring consistency and reducing cognitive load.

Quality assurance and continuous improvement form the operational backbone of OctoAcme's delivery process. All work follows a pull request workflow with small PRs (≤400 lines preferred), automated CI testing, security scanning, and required approvals before merging. Testing occurs at multiple levels: unit tests for new logic, integration tests for component interactions, and end-to-end smoke tests for critical flows. After each sprint, release, or incident, the team conducts timeboxed retrospectives (45-75 minutes) that capture what went well, what needs improvement, and 2-3 prioritized action items with clear owners and due dates. This commitment to learning and iteration—supported by velocity tracking, burndown metrics, and observable dashboards—enables OctoAcme to deliver reliable software while continuously refining their processes based on team feedback and measured outcomes.
